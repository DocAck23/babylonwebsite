Executive Summary

The Power Crisis Is Now

Global data center power consumption is projected to reach 96 GW by 2026 — nearly double 2023 levels — with AI workloads consuming over 40% of that load. In the US alone, roughly 50 GW of new data center projects were announced in 2025.

The grid cannot keep up. PJM Interconnection warns of a 49 GW US generation shortfall by 2028. Half of all global data center projects face delays due to power limitations. Tech giants are scrambling: Meta committed $1.6B for on-site gas generation, Microsoft is partnering with Chevron on a 5 GW gas plant, and Google is building a 933 MW facility in Texas.

New heavy-duty gas turbines are virtually unobtainable. Lead times have stretched to 5–7 years. GE Vernova’s 80 GW backlog extends through 2029, with reservations expected to sell out through 2030. Turbine prices have risen 195% since 2019. Only three OEMs worldwide produce these machines.
